Hello! Welcome to Trailblazer.


This app is meant to manage everything in life.
Sounds ambitious, but hear me out.
The basic workflow of the app is as follows:
  • Add/remove TRAILS and SUBTRAILS
  • Check off items as you go through the day
  • EVALUATE the day after it ends
  • On the side, track TASKS and use the NOTEBOOK

Let’s get started!

BASICS

  • Main panel Trails are here
  • Add Trail
  • Categories
  • Tasks
  • Notebook
  • BLAZE panel

TRAILS

  • Name Edit directly
  • Edit Opens up subpanel for editing subTrails
  • Streak How well you’ve been sticking to the Trail
  • Delete
  • Trailboxes subTrail names popup on hover, toggle for completion

NOTE: subTrail cards work the same way as Trails
BUT a trail two levels deep cannot have subtrails under it.
IE: Math -> HW -> Fourier Series – Fourier Series can’t have subtrails.

ADVICE

This panel contains what I personally do with the app - ultimately, you decide how Trailblazer helps you.

GENERAL WORKFLOW

HABITS

I tend to do Habits first - they get me started during the day (or the afternoon when I'm tired). For this reason, my Habits are generally quick, routine, rote, and less than 5 minutes. It should be something you can feasibly keep up with without too much pressure. Examples: Clean Room, Quick Excercise, Meditate, etc.

WORK + HOBBIES

After you're a bit more focused, you'll be able to work on more complex things. Trailblazer treats Work and Hobbies as projects - they have subTrails for ease of planning. Obviously, prioritize work with deadlines first - I recommend NOT involving tasks or specific dates for this. If there's an important deadline, chances are you're well aware of it already. But then again, that's up to you.
I use the subTrail mode for a Work/Hobby, which allows me to focus, as well as quickly add subTrails as needed. Utilize subTrails - they're meant to visualize the entire Trail you're working on, for the sake of better awareness and time allocation.

TASKS

Throughout this process, in a parallel fashion, you want to work on your Tasks a little. Tasks should strictly be quick one time events - handing off something, payments, etc.
I use the toggle button to remind myself whether this Task can be worked on today - I regularly check back to make sure I don't miss things.
